Understanding Acidity: When Is It a Problem?

Stomach acid (hydrochloric acid) is essential for digestion — it activates digestive enzymes, kills pathogens in food, and enables protein digestion. The problem is not acid itself but where it goes and how much is produced:

  • Hyperacidity: Excessive acid production beyond what digestion requires
  • GERD: Acid escapes into the oesophagus due to a weakened lower oesophageal sphincter (LES)
  • Peptic ulcers: Acid damages the stomach lining, creating painful sores
  • Gastritis: Inflammation of the stomach lining from excess acid or H. pylori infection

⚠️ The PPI Problem: Prolonged use of PPIs (omeprazole/pantoprazole beyond 8 weeks) causes: magnesium deficiency, Vitamin B12 malabsorption, 2x increased risk of C. difficile infection, chronic kidney disease risk, increased fracture risk, and severe rebound acidity when stopped. Millions of Indians are on PPIs for years — with no plan to stop. Ayurvedic treatment heals the stomach so medication is no longer needed.

Signs You Have Chronic Acidity or GERD

  • Burning sensation in the chest or throat after meals (heartburn)
  • Sour or bitter taste in the mouth, especially in the morning
  • Nausea after eating, particularly with fatty or spicy foods
  • Feeling of fullness and bloating even after small meals
  • Frequent belching or hiccups
  • Chronic cough or throat clearing (acid reaching the larynx)
  • Hoarse voice, especially in the morning
  • Pain or discomfort in the upper abdomen (epigastric pain)
  • Symptoms worsening when lying down or bending over

Dietary Rules for Acidity — What to Eat and Avoid

Cooling Foods That Reduce Acidity

  • Banana: Natural antacid properties — contains sucralfate-like compounds that coat the stomach lining
  • Coconut water: Alkalising, cooling, instantly soothes heartburn
  • Buttermilk (chaach): Probiotic and alkalising — the traditional Indian post-meal digestive for good reason
  • Cold milk: Provides immediate heartburn relief through calcium-based neutralisation
  • Cucumber: Water-rich, alkalising, soothes stomach inflammation
  • Fennel seeds (saunf): Reduces LES relaxation, cooling digestive effect

Foods That Trigger Acidity — Avoid These

  • Citrus fruits (direct acid addition to stomach content)
  • Tomatoes and tomato-based foods (both acidic and LES-relaxing)
  • Coffee and strong tea (stimulate excess acid production)
  • Alcohol (directly damages stomach lining and relaxes LES)
  • Chocolate (theobromins relax LES — increases reflux)
  • Peppermint (paradoxically worsens GERD despite feeling cooling)
  • All fried and fatty foods (delay gastric emptying, increase acid exposure time)
  • Onion and garlic (relax LES in most acidity-prone individuals)

The Three Most Important Behavioural Rules

  1. Don't eat within 3 hours of bedtime: Lying down with food in the stomach is the most common cause of nighttime reflux
  2. Raise the head end of your bed 15 cm: Gravity is your best friend against reflux — elevation prevents acid from reaching the oesophagus while sleeping
  3. Eat slowly and chew thoroughly: Rushed eating triggers more acid secretion and swallows more air — both worsen acidity
